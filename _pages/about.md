Embedded Systems Engineer focused on real-time system testing, validation, and simulation-driven debugging

I work on large-scale industrial SMT systems, focusing on system-level validation, real-time behavior analysis, and deep debugging of C/C++ embedded applications. My work involves diagnosing complex issues across firmware, middleware, and hardware layers, often using simulation-based workflows to reproduce and resolve system-level defects.

With over 3 years of experience in embedded systems, I specialize in:
- System-level debugging and root cause analysis  
- Real-time system validation in RTOS-based environments  
- Integration testing across software and hardware layers  
- Simulation-driven testing (Software-in-the-Loop)  
- Translating system requirements into structured test cases  

My engineering approach is grounded in understanding how systems actually behave at runtime — not just how they are designed. I focus on execution flow, timing behavior, and interactions between components to ensure reliability in complex environments.

I am particularly interested in:
- Autonomous and real-time systems validation  
- Simulation-based testing and verification  
- Performance-aware embedded software design  
- Debugging large and legacy C/C++ codebases  
- Core computer science fundamentals and system architecture  

This site serves as a structured record of my engineering work and learning. I document real-world debugging cases, system behavior analysis, testing strategies, and architectural insights — with an emphasis on clarity, reproducibility, and long-term maintainability.

I am actively exploring opportunities in autonomous systems testing, where I can apply my experience in real-time system validation and simulation-driven debugging to complex, safety-critical environments.
